Minji Heo

During my Master’s studies, my research has focused on analyzing the robustness of deepfake detectors under real-world scenarios, particularly with moiré artifacts from screen capture and multi-step compositional manipulations. These experiences have strengthened my background in computer vision, representation learning, and media forensics.

Looking ahead, I am particularly interested in pursuing a Ph.D. in computer graphics and physics-based 3D reconstruction, aiming to develop trustworthy, physically grounded models that connect visual realism with reliability.

Publications

Through the Lens: Benchmarking Deepfake Detectors Against Moiré-Induced Distortions
Razaib Tariq*, Minji Heo*, Simon S. Woo, Shahroz Tariq
Neural Information Processing Systems Benchmark Track (NeurIPS), 2025 (accepted)
PDF arXiv BibTeX

We introduce DMF, a screen-recaptured benchmark of 12,832 videos (35.64 h), evaluate 15 state-of-the-art deepfake detectors, and find that Moiré artifacts result in accuracy reductions of up to 25.4%, while demoiréing can further degrade performance by up to 16%.

FakeChain: Exposing Shallow Cues in Multi-Step Deepfake Detection
Minji Heo, Simon S. Woo
ACM International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management (CIKM), 2025 (accepted)
PDF arXiv BibTeX Code

We analyze final-step dominance and frequency overwriting across single- and multi-step pipelines, showing detector reliance on last-stage artifacts.

Self-Disclosure of Mental Health via Deepfakes
Jiyoung Lee, Christopher Michael Dobmeier, Minji Heo, Simon S. Woo
International Conference for Computational Social Science (IC2S2), 2025
PDF

Tests how self-deepfakes affect affective resistance and intentions to seek support in mental-health messaging.
